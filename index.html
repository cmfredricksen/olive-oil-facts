Nutrition

The health benefits of olive oil are extensive, and new positive attributes are being discovered all the time. One prominent cardiologist recommends at least two tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil each day to enjoy the many ways olive oil can be beneficial to your health and well being.

    One tablespoon(13.5g) of Extra Virgin Olive Oil contains:
  • Calories: 119
  • Fat: 13.5g (21% of DV)
  • Saturated Fat: 2g (9% of DV)
  • Carbohydrates: 0
  • Fibers: 0
  • Proteins: 0
  • Vitamin E: 1.9mg (10% of DV)
  • Vitamin K: (10% of DV)

Cooking

High quality EVOO [extra virgin olive oil] is an excellent choice for cooking. High quality EVOO has a smoke point well above the standard temperatures required for cooking, and its resistance to oxidation is higher than most cooking oils due to the antioxidant and mono-unsaturated fat content. The smoke point of a good extra-virgin olive oil is 210 °C (420 °F). The smoke point is higher in good extra-virgin olive oil and lower in low-quality virgin olive oil.--Wikipedia Olive Oil
